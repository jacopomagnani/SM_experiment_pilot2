The correct answer is: 'Player B may earn any of the following amounts of points: 7, 1 or -3.'


Explanation

Group 1
Player Player's Multiplier Player's Bid
A 9 3
B 2 3
C 6 3
Group 2
Player Player's Multiplier Player's Bid
D 5 2
E 2 12

Players A, B and C bid the same value and therefore the computer will randomly determine their ranking.

In Group 2, the highest bidder is player E and the second-highest bidder is player D.

If the computer determines B is the highest bidder in his group, then he will be paired with player E and his score will be:

(B's multiplier)×(E's multiplier)−(B's bid) = 2×2−3 = 4-3=1

If the computer determines B is the second-highest bidder in his group, then he will be paired with player D and his score will be:

(B's multiplier)×(D's multiplier)−(B's bid) = 2×5−3 = 10-3=7

If the computer determines B is the lowest bidder in his group, then he will be unpaired and his score will be:

0−(B's bid) =0−3 =-3

Thus, player B may earn any of these three scores, depending on the random choice of the computer.
