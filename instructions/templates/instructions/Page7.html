BIDS
{% endblock %} {% block content %}
  • You cannot choose with whom to form a pair.
  • However, you can try to influence the way in which you are paired to others by submitting a bid.
  • A bid is a number between 0 and 50. Each bid is subtracted from your points at the end of the round. If you do not want to bid, then you can choose to bid zero.
  • After all players in a market have submitted their bids, the computer will rank the bids of the three players in Group 1 from highest to lowest.
  • Similarly, the computer will rank the bids of the three players in Group 2 from highest to lowest.
  • The computer will then pair players whose bids have the same ranking, that is:
    • the player who submitted the highest bid in Group 1 is paired with the player who submitted the highest bid in Group 2.
    • the player who submitted the second-highest bid in Group 1 is paired with the player who submitted the second-highest bid in Group 2.
    • the player who submitted the lowest bid in Group 1 is paired with the player who submitted the lowest bid in Group 2.
  • The computer will randomly break any tie: if two or three players in the same group have submitted the same bid, the computer will randomly rank their bids.
