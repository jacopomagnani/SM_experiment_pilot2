The correct answer is: 'Player B will earn either 12 points or 4 points.'


Explanation

Group 1
Player Player's Multiplier Player's Bid
A 2 10
B 4 8
C 6 6
Group 2
Player Player's Multiplier Player's Bid
D 3 0
E 5 0

Player B bid 8 and the other players in his group bid 10 and 6. Thus, player B is the second-highest bidder in his group.

In Group 2, both players bid the same value. Therefore, the computer will randomly determine the ranking of their bids.

If the computer determines D is the second-highest bidder in his group, then player B will be paired with player D and his score will be:

(B's multiplier)×(D's multiplier)−(B's bid) = 4×3−8 = 12-8=4

If the computer determines E is the second-highest bidder in his group, then player B will be paired with player E and his score will be:

(B's multiplier)×(E's multiplier)−(B's bid) = 4×5−6 = 20-6=12

Thus, player B may earn either of these two scores, depending on the random choice of the computer. However, player B has no chance to earn -8 points. That would happen only if both A and C bid more than B, in which case B would remain unpaired.
